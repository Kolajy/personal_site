After working as a software engineer for several years, I realized something slightly embarrassing: I've treated the operating system as a black box. Sure, I know how to use files, threads, and processes in high-level languages, but when it comes to *how* the scheduler coordinates CPU time or how virtual memory maps to physical RAM, my mental models are fuzzy.

This month, I'm embarking on a journey to relearn the fundamentals of operating systems. I'm starting from the absolute basics, trying to dismantle the magic and understand what actually happens under the hood.

Here is my roadmap for the next 30 days:
1. Processes, threads, and CPU scheduling.
2. Memory management, paging, and virtual memory.
3. Concurrency, mutexes, and deadlocks.
4. Storage, file systems, and hardware devices.

Let's see where this road takes us.
